# World Brief vs. Tool Surface — Fit Audit

*How well does the rpg-mcp + Naruto 5e tool surface actually deliver on the claims in [docs/bastion/04-world-brief.md](./04-world-brief.md)? An adversarially-verified mapping, claim by claim, with code citations.*

**Audit method:** four parallel auditors split the brief by theme (geography/wall/outpost; population/summoning/cohorts; System-as-physics; powers-collisions-time); each finding then survived an independent verifier who defaulted to `is_real=true` unless they found the auditor misread the code.

---

## Headline

The brief's central conceit — *a System that is the neutral physics of the world, addressed by petition and answered truthfully* — is **already shipped**. The router, the audit trail, the seeded dice, the petition-discipline of every Zod gate: the engine *is* the System the brief describes. That is the strongest fit in the codebase. But the load-bearing **content** of Bastion — the walled city as a first-class entity, the summoning order, the cohort, the imported soul's *origin*, the cross-universe physics pack, the enduring siege, the reclaimed outpost, the eternal war that just stopped being eternal — lives almost entirely in prose. The schema does not know any of it. The keystone gap is **cross-universe physics primitives** in rpg-mcp itself: the Naruto 5e tree has already shipped the right shape (`ResourceDefSchema`, `ClassificationDefSchema`, content-pack registry), so the work is to port, not to invent. And one open question hangs over everything: the brief insists time is **irreversible** — the engine's audit log records every commit, but no migration yet forbids retroactive UPDATEs, so the irreversibility is a *convention* the System enforces by tool-discipline, not a substrate invariant.

## Strong fits

- **"An intelligence that is the physics of the world"** — [SYSTEM.md:11](../../SYSTEM.md) declares the operator as "the neutral runtime of the world"; [SYSTEM.md:62-80](../../SYSTEM.md) maps every spec concept onto a concrete tool family. The 29 consolidated tools enumerated at [src/server/consolidated/index.ts:1-54](../../src/server/consolidated/index.ts) *are* the physics laws.
- **"Not a menu, not a companion character — the law of reality itself, with a query interface"** — [src/utils/action-router.ts:43-79](../../src/utils/action-router.ts) defines `ActionDefinition` as a Zod schema plus handler. No persona. [src/utils/fuzzy-enum.ts:133-197](../../src/utils/fuzzy-enum.ts) `matchAction` is the literal query interface: free-form input → normalized → exact / alias / Levenshtein.
- **"Asking reality a question, and reality answers truthfully"** — every call passes through `schema.safeParse` at [src/utils/action-router.ts:182-203](../../src/utils/action-router.ts); on failure `formatValidationError` ([action-router.ts:358-380](../../src/utils/action-router.ts)) returns the Zod issues verbatim, not a glossed answer. [src/server/audit.ts:40-56](../../src/server/audit.ts) `detectSoftError` inspects the returned envelope for falsified "success" and records the truthful outcome.
- **"It does not lie about the numbers — because the numbers aren't its to invent"** — [src/math/dice.ts:1-11](../../src/math/dice.ts) uses seedrandom with an explicit seed stored on the result; [src/server/consolidated/math-manage.ts:96-123](../../src/server/consolidated/math-manage.ts) persists every roll via `CalculationRepository` with the seed at line 107. [SYSTEM.md:25](../../SYSTEM.md): *"Every mechanical fact you assert must have been produced by a tool call."*
- **"It does not volunteer; it answers exactly what's asked"** — [src/utils/action-router.ts:146-203](../../src/utils/action-router.ts) executes only the single handler matched. No broadcast. No proactive suggestion. The audit logger writes asynchronously in `finally{}` and never modifies the returned response ([audit.ts:66-99](../../src/server/audit.ts)). The four meta tools (`search_tools`, `load_tool_schema`, `subscribe/unsubscribe`) exist precisely so the LLM has to *ask* for schema and events — pull, not push.
- **"The neutral runtime of the universe"** — the router has no agent, no preference, no scheduler-initiated invocation. The fuzzy matcher ([fuzzy-enum.ts:133-197](../../src/utils/fuzzy-enum.ts)) is symmetric across all valid actions. The will / write-access firewall is implemented: persona lives in [src/server/consolidated/agent-manage.ts](../../src/server/consolidated/agent-manage.ts), never in the router.
- **"He can petition the System ... but he can't force outcomes"** — [SYSTEM.md:25-29](../../SYSTEM.md) codifies the two-gate petition model; *even the maker* routes through it. [action-router.ts:182-186](../../src/utils/action-router.ts) Zod `safeParse` is non-negotiable; invalid petitions produce no commit. There is no admin / escape-hatch tool in [src/server/consolidated/index.ts:1-54](../../src/server/consolidated/index.ts).

## Partial fits — real but incomplete

### "A noir detective, a shōnen fighter, a hard-SF engineer, refugees from other isekai worlds"

**What works.** The engine accepts arbitrary strings for `race` and `class` ([src/schema/character.ts:38-40](../../src/schema/character.ts) — "any string allowed (Human, Elf, Dragonborn, Mousefolk...)" and "Flexible character class - allows any string"). Agent prompt slices ([src/schema/agent.ts:39-47](../../src/schema/agent.ts)) — persona, directive, secrets, narrative_feed, recent, character_state, custom — let the DM hand a summoned soul any personality blob. So *by convention* you can have a "Noir Detective" character with a freeform persona today.

**What's missing.** No `sourceUniverse` field. No `archetype` field. No way for the engine to enforce or query "one detective from world A, one shōnen fighter from world B" — the melting-pot is in string content, not structured data. `character-manage` cannot filter `list` by source universe; `rite_manage` cannot record which universes a cohort drew from (because neither exists yet).

**Gap-closer.** Add `sourceUniverse: string` and `archetype: string` (free-form, e.g. `noir_detective`, `shonen_ninja`, `hard_sf_engineer`) to `CharacterSchema`. Optionally a soft enum of well-known genres for fuzzy filter. ETA: half a day for schema + repo column + migration; existing freeform `race`/`class` already proves the pattern works.

### "Nothing gets nerfed, buffed, or flattened into one house ruleset"

**What works.** Character `resourcePools: Record<string, {current, max, lastRefilledAt}>` at [src/schema/character.ts:90-94](../../src/schema/character.ts) is the generic escape hatch the brief's physics model needs — a soul can in principle carry an arbitrary named resource pool. The Naruto 5e tree has already proven the shape: [Naruto 5e/packages/engine/src/domain/resource.ts:39-62](../../../Naruto%205e/packages/engine/src/domain/resource.ts) defines `ResourceDefSchema` (named pools — chakra is just one) and `ClassificationDefSchema` (nin/gen/tai/buki) with `castingAbility` strings like `"str/dex"` that base 5e cannot represent. [Naruto 5e/packages/engine/src/content.ts:108-129](../../../Naruto%205e/packages/engine/src/content.ts) lets a content pack register its own resources and classifications.

**What's missing.** rpg-mcp itself is **5e-locked** on the cast path. [CharacterSchema](../../src/schema/character.ts) declares `spellSlots` as the closed `SpellSlotsSchema` ([src/schema/spell.ts:172-182](../../src/schema/spell.ts) — hard-coded `level1`..`level9` 5e slots) plus Warlock-only `pactMagicSlots`. `spellcastingAbility` is locked to `{intelligence | wisdom | charisma}`. The combat-action `cast_spell` path at [src/server/consolidated/combat-action.ts:60-68](../../src/server/consolidated/combat-action.ts) keys off `spellSlots`/`pactMagicSlots` only — never off `resourcePools`. So the substrate is *almost* universe-neutral and the cast path is *not*.

**Gap-closer.** Port the Naruto Phase-A primitives wholesale: add `src/schema/resource.ts` (mirror `ResourceDefSchema` + `ClassificationDefSchema`); add `originPack: string` and `physics: { resources: string[], classifications: string[] }` to `CharacterSchema`; ship `physics_pack_manage` (register/list resources and classifications); teach `combat-action.cast_spell` and `improvisation_manage.synthesize` to debit `character.resourcePools[id]` when a spell/jutsu declares a non-5e resource. Deprecate-but-keep `spellSlots` as the canonical 5e physics pack. **ETA: 3-4 days for schema + handler wiring; the math/dice surface does not change.** This is the keystone gap.

### "Time in Bastion is honest and irreversible"

**What works.** The audit substrate enforces the *honest* half completely: [src/server/audit.ts:81-93](../../src/server/audit.ts) records args + result + success + duration on every tool call, [math-manage.ts:96-123](../../src/server/consolidated/math-manage.ts) persists every dice roll with seed, and SYSTEM.md:25 forbids direct world-state mutation. Time advancement runs through `turn_manage` and `rest_manage` — a player cannot magically extend a day without producing a tool-call trail.

**What's missing.** The *irreversibility* half is enforced by **convention**, not by substrate. There is no append-only constraint on the SQLite tables; a sufficiently privileged caller (or a bug in any consolidated handler) can issue an UPDATE against `characters`, `world_state`, `narrative_notes`, etc. and erase prior history. The brief's "the ledger only moves forward" is true of the audit log but not strictly of the world-state tables.

**Gap-closer.** Either (a) add a `world_events` event-sourced table where every state change is an append-only row and current state is a projection — the same shape `diplomacy_events` already uses at [src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts:94-102](../../src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts) — or (b) ship a SQLite trigger that copies row pre-images into a shadow `_history` table on UPDATE. The cheapest fix is (b) plus a tool-level "no DELETE / no UPDATE of `created_at`" lint. ETA: 1-2 days for the shadow-table approach.

## Gaps — the brief promises what the engine cannot yet do

### "Bastion is the last walled city of humanity"

**Missing.** [WorldSchema](../../src/schema/world.ts) (lines 3-23) has only `{id, name, seed, width, height, environment, createdAt, updatedAt}` — no civilizational scope, no uniqueness flag. [StructureType](../../src/schema/structure.ts) (lines 3-11) enumerates exactly `{CITY, TOWN, VILLAGE, CASTLE, RUINS, DUNGEON, TEMPLE}` — no `WALLED_CITY`. [POICategorySchema](../../src/schema/poi.ts) has `fortification` for castles/forts/towers but no walled-city subtype and no `is_last`/`is_capital_of_humanity` marker. The codebase's "wall" references at [src/server/terrain-patterns.ts:35-99](../../src/server/terrain-patterns.ts) and [src/server/handlers/combat-handlers.ts:553-555](../../src/server/handlers/combat-handlers.ts) are tactical combat obstacles inside encounter grids, not city perimeters.

**Gap-closer.** Add `StructureType.WALLED_CITY` and extend `StructureSchema` with `{wallSegments: WallSegment[], isPerimeter: bool, civilizationRole: 'capital'|'outpost'|'lost'}`. Add a world-level `WorldDoctrineSchema` (last-city flag, established date, summoning order) and a `world_manage.set_doctrine` action. ETA: 1-2 days for schema + migration + handler.

### "The city holds against an eternal siege"

**Missing.** [strategy_manage](../../src/server/consolidated/strategy-manage.ts) (lines 25-90) models nation-vs-nation diplomacy via `create_nation` / `claim_region` / `resolve_turn` — no SIEGE state. [ConflictResolver.resolveRegionConflict](../../src/engine/strategy/conflict-resolver.ts) (lines 14-87) treats a contested region as one d20-style battle per turn (`damage = max(10, diff/2)`, ownership flips when `controlLevel <= 0`) — a snapshot fight, not enduring pressure. [TurnProcessor.processTurn](../../src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts) (lines 15-26) runs `economy → conflict → consumption` with no continuous-pressure phase. No "Hell" / demonic-invasion faction template exists; a grep across `src/` for `siege|hell|demon|invasion|besieg` returns only a cosmetic `Broken Siege Engine` prop at [combat-handlers.ts:3397](../../src/server/handlers/combat-handlers.ts).

**Gap-closer.** Add `SiegeStateSchema { besiegedSettlementId, attackingFactionId, pressure: 0-100, wallIntegrity: 0-100, duration_days }` persisted alongside Region; extend `TurnProcessor` with a `processSiege` phase that ticks pressure each turn and degrades `wallIntegrity`, emits `SIEGE_BREACH` events. Best version: model Hell as a permanent off-map adversarial Nation with `publicIntent='annihilation'` and an "unkillable adversary" flag on Nation. ETA: 3-5 days.

### "The city has reclaimed an outpost outside the wall"

**Missing.** No `OUTPOST` `StructureType` ([structure.ts:3-11](../../src/schema/structure.ts)). No `status: 'held'|'lost'|'contested'|'reclaimed'` on `StructureSchema` or `RegionSchema` ([region.ts:3-15](../../src/schema/region.ts)). `RegionSchema.controlLevel` plus `ownerNationId` flipping in [turn-processor.ts:57-92](../../src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts) can encode "Bastion took region X" — but the only event types emitted are `REGION_CLAIMED` and `REGION_CONQUERED` ([turn-processor.ts:64,97](../../src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts)); no `RECLAIMED`. [POIDiscoveryStateSchema](../../src/schema/poi.ts) (lines 37-43) enumerates only `unknown/rumored/discovered/explored/mapped` — no `lost` or `reclaimed` transition.

**Gap-closer.** Add `StructureType.OUTPOST` plus a `status` field on `StructureSchema` (or `RegionSchema`) and a `reclaimedAt: datetime`. Extend `strategy_manage` with action `reclaim_outpost(worldId, structureId, byNationId)` that writes the status flip and logs a `RECLAIMED` event via `diplomacyRepo.logEvent` ([turn-processor.ts:94-102](../../src/engine/strategy/turn-processor.ts)). Mirror in `POIDiscoveryStateSchema` by adding `lost` and `reclaimed`. ETA: 1 day.

### "Something that everyone deemed impossible just happened"

**Missing.** Nothing in the codebase encodes "impossibility" or shared world-belief state. [NationSchema](../../src/schema/nation.ts) (lines 11-38) has `privateMemory` + `publicIntent` (key/value bag and freeform string) but no canonical world-axiom table. The closed `NationEventSchema.eventType` enum at [src/schema/diplomacy.ts:32-33](../../src/schema/diplomacy.ts) has no `PARADIGM_SHIFT`-equivalent. [narrative_manage](../../src/server/consolidated/narrative-manage.ts) (lines 23-79) is a freeform note system — useful as a story log but cannot adjudicate "impossibility is now possible" as engine state.

**Gap-closer.** Lightweight: add a `PARADIGM_SHIFT` event type plus a `canonical_moment` narrative entry — the System can commit "the impossible thing happened" without inventing axiom state. Full version: add a `WorldAxiomSchema { worldId, axiom: string, status: 'held'|'broken', brokenAt? }` and a `world_manage.break_axiom` action that fires a hook to NPC memory invalidation across all NPCs in that world. ETA: 0.5 day lightweight; 2-3 days for the full axiom-tracking version.

### "The eternal war is suddenly not so eternal"

**Missing.** No `War` entity. [NationSchema.relations](../../src/schema/nation.ts) (lines 26-30) has only `{opinion, alliance, truceUntil}` per pair — no "state of war" enum, no war start/end timestamps, no duration tracking. `WAR_DECLARED` / `PEACE_SIGNED` exist as event-type strings in [diplomacy.ts:32-33](../../src/schema/diplomacy.ts) but are transient log entries, not persisted multi-turn entities. The engine can emit "a war event happened this turn" but cannot observe "this war was eternal."

**Gap-closer.** Add `WarSchema { id, worldId, sideA, sideB, startedAt, endedAt?, duration_turns, isEnduring: bool, narrativeArc? }` and a `WarRepository`. Extend `NationSchema.relations` to reference `activeWarIds`. Add `strategy_manage` actions: `declare_war`, `end_war`, `get_war_status`. Then "the eternal war is suddenly not so eternal" becomes: `war.endedAt = now → emit WAR_ENDED with prior duration`. ETA: 2-3 days.

### "Almost no one is native"

**Missing.** [CharacterSchema](../../src/schema/character.ts) (lines 10-110) has no `origin`, `nativeToBastion`, `sourceUniverse`, `arrivedAt`, or `arrivedInCohortId`. `character-manage` `CreateSchema` ([character-manage.ts:65-88](../../src/server/consolidated/character-manage.ts)) accepts `background` and `alignment` and `handleCreate` ([lines 197-223](../../src/server/consolidated/character-manage.ts)) sets them on the in-memory character, but the [characters table DDL](../../src/storage/migrations.ts) and the [CharacterRepository INSERT](../../src/storage/repos/character.repo.ts) (lines 13-26) have no `background`, `alignment`, or origin columns — those values are **silently dropped on persistence**. `batch_manage` and `spawn_manage` have no origin concept either.

**Gap-closer.** Add `origin: { universe: string; native: boolean; arrivedAt?: string; arrivedInCohortId?: string }` to `CharacterSchema` with a corresponding migration. Plumb through `character_manage.CreateSchema`, `npc_manage.NewCreateSchema`, `batch_manage.BatchCharacterSchema`, `spawn_manage.buildCharacter`. Add a `nativeToBastion` filter on `character_manage.list`. ETA: 1 day. (Also fix the silent-drop bug for `background`/`alignment` while there.)

### "A holy order whose doctrine is summoning"

**Missing.** No `summon_manage`, `rite_manage`, or `void_manage` tool. The string "summon" appears in code only as (a) a generic spell `type` enum value at [src/schema/spell.ts:108](../../src/schema/spell.ts), (b) a `SpellEffectTypeSchema` enum value at [src/schema/improvisation.ts:242](../../src/schema/improvisation.ts), (c) a `spawn` alias in `combat-manage` ([line 486](../../src/server/consolidated/combat-manage.ts)), and (d) a TODO stub at [src/engine/magic/spell-resolver.ts:281-285](../../src/engine/magic/spell-resolver.ts): `case 'summon': { // TODO(high): Implement summoning; result.success = true; break; }`. The closest mechanic is `spawn_manage` ([spawn-manage.ts](../../src/server/consolidated/spawn-manage.ts)) which builds characters from creature templates (goblin, orc, bandit per ALIASES at lines 144-162) — not from fictional-universe imports. No religion, faith, order, doctrine, rite, ritual, or sacrament concept exists anywhere in `src/schema/`.

**Gap-closer.** Build `rite_manage` with actions: `perform_summoning_rite` (records the rite event, ties it to a Summoner agent + cohort id), `list_rites`, `get_rite`. Add a `summoned_souls` table keyed by `(characterId, cohortId, sourceUniverse, summonerId, riteTimestamp)`. Build `faction_manage` (or `order_manage`) with `FactionSchema { id, name, kind: 'holy_order'|'guild'|'criminal', doctrine: string, members: characterId[] }`. Tie rite events to the holy-order faction. Spell-system summoning (`spell-resolver.ts:281`) stays distinct — that is an in-combat conjuration, not the doctrinal rite. ETA: 3-4 days for `rite_manage` + `faction_manage` + schema + migration + tests.

### "He summons cohorts, selected on purpose"

**Missing.** Grep `cohort` across `src/` returns **zero matches**. The concept is heavily documented in `docs/bastion/` ([00-campaign-bible.md:709](./00-campaign-bible.md), [03-resolved-decisions.md:19](./03-resolved-decisions.md), `bastion-deliverables.md:54-126`) but no schema, no table, no tool action, no field references it. [party_manage](../../src/server/consolidated/party-manage.ts) exists as a grouping mechanism, but a party is a player-formed adventuring group — not a summoned-together cohort with a shared rite origin. [agent_manage](../../src/server/consolidated/agent-manage.ts) creates a single LLM-driven agent bound 1:1 to a character ([line 100-111](../../src/server/consolidated/agent-manage.ts)). [batch_manage](../../src/server/consolidated/batch-manage.ts) has `create_characters` (max 20) and `create_npcs` (max 50) — many-at-once creation works, but there is no `summonedBy` link, no `cohortId`, no record that "Mnehmos chose these by name and nature." Grep `Mnehmos` in `src/` returns zero hits.

**Gap-closer.** Add `src/schema/cohort.ts` with `CohortSchema { id, name, summonedAt, summonerAgentId, riteId, memberCharacterIds: string[], sourceTheme? }`. Add `cohort_manage` with `create / get / list / add_member / remove_member / dissolve`. Tie `rite_manage.perform_cohort_summoning` to atomically create a cohort row plus the summoned characters. Migration adds `cohorts` and `cohort_members` tables. This is the load-bearing piece for the whole population/summoning chunk — without cohorts in code, "selected on purpose" lives only in the DM's head. ETA: 2 days.

## Absences that are correct

Section 22-28 of the brief enumerates **deliberately open questions**. The engine should not yet model them; their absence is the design.

- **"Is the summoning salvation or atrocity?"** — should remain prose, not schema. The brief says "Mnehmos's character — and the morality of the whole enterprise — is supposed to emerge from his choices, one act at a time, not be declared." No `moralAlignment` field on `RiteSchema` is correct. [SYSTEM.md:131](../../SYSTEM.md) keeps "Does the System want anything?" deliberately unanswered.
- **"Does the System want anything?"** — confirmed absent: [src/utils/action-router.ts:146-203](../../src/utils/action-router.ts) is a pure dispatcher with no scheduler-initiated handler invocation, no goal state, no preference loop. No "System will" surface should be added.
- **"What does the reclaimed outpost mean — why now, after a century?"** — should not have a `causeOfReclamation` enum. The mystery is the content; if the engine had a structured field for it, that field would have to be answered before play begins, which the brief explicitly forbids.

## Methodology + caveats

Four parallel auditors split the brief by theme and produced structured findings; an independent verifier then defaulted every finding to `is_real=true` unless they found the auditor had misread the code. Every citation in this document was re-checked against the file at the cited line. This is a **point-in-time snapshot**: the engine is moving — at least two workflows are in flight (the harden-mcp-server branch, ongoing agent-module work) and the Naruto 5e tree is itself active. The biggest single source of drift would be a port of the resource/classification primitives landing in `src/schema/` between now and the next audit. **Re-audit after Chapter 1 commits**, after the cohort/rite/faction tool families ship, or after any change to the spell-resolver `cast` path.
