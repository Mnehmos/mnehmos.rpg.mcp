# Sebastopyr — A Note on the City's Name

*Lifted from the canon gap-fill record. For the full reasoning and the rejected alternatives, see the bundle's `canon-gap-fill` payload.*

## The Name

**Sebastopyr** — pronounced *seh-BAS-toh-peer* in formal liturgical Old Tongue, *SEB-stuh-peer* in the clipped Pyrian street dialect. The city is almost universally shortened to **the Pyr** in everyday speech. Citizens are **Pyrians**; the curtain wall is **the Pyric Wall**.

## Etymology

Sebastopyr fuses the Old-Tongue root *sebast-* (the martyr-name; "the venerable one who endured the arrows and did not die of them, then was martyred anyway") with *-pyr*, the Old-Tongue suffix for "wall" or "ward-stone" — literally a watchfire-stone set in the curtain wall of a holy keep. In its first reading the name means **"The Wall Named For The Enduring One."**

But the same root *sebast-* in the older liturgical case carries a bitter secondary sense: *the one who endured for nothing* — because the saint endured the arrows only to be clubbed to death in the second martyrdom. The founders chose the name in sixth-century court Old-Tongue, and the scholiasts have argued for six hundred years which sense was intended. The Cathedrate's official position is that **both senses were intended at once** — that the city's name is a deliberate paradox the founders left lit for their descendants. At least one suppressed gloss in the archive reads the name as a confession, set in stone by men who already knew the war could not be won and named their wall accordingly.

## The Daily Forgetting

The shortening to **the Pyr** is the most important fact about the name. Almost no one says the full word in casual speech. The full word carries the buried second meaning — *the one who endured for nothing, set in stone* — and the casual shortening lets every Pyrian forget, every day, what the second half of the name actually confesses. The founders may have intended this too. The Cathedrate has never ruled.

## Why Not the Alternatives (Brief)

Five other names were considered: **Sebastrian Quietus** (too on-the-nose, players would shorten to "Sebastian"), **Aspex Sebastine** (mouth-feel too clipped, foregrounded watching over enduring), **Carcerost** (committed too hard to the futility reading; the church would not name its capital "Prison-place"), **Vigilarn** (dropped the saint entirely), and **Adamantium-of-the-Arrows** (a real-world word imported as if it were original Old Tongue).
